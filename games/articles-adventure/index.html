Articles: A, An, The, And Zero Article

Articles are small words before nouns, but they carry a big meaning. They tell your listener whether the noun is general or specific.

A useful teacher question is: Do we know exactly which noun? If the answer is no, you often need a/an for one singular countable noun. If the answer is yes, you often need the. If you are speaking generally about plural or uncountable nouns, you often use no article.

1. Use a/an for one general thing

Use a or an when you mean one thing, but not a specific one. It is one of many possible things.

  • I need a pen. Any pen is okay.
  • She is a teacher. This tells us her job.
  • Paris is an interesting city. This describes Paris.

2. A or an depends on sound

Use a before consonant sounds and an before vowel sounds. Do not only look at the first letter. Listen to the first sound.

  • a university because it starts with a /y/ sound.
  • a European country because it starts with a /y/ sound.
  • an hour because the h is silent.
  • an honest man because the h is silent.

3. Use the when it is specific

Use the when the speaker and listener know which thing we mean. The noun is not just one of many anymore.

  • Close the door. We know which door.
  • I saw a dog. The dog was black. Second mention makes it specific.
  • Where are the kids? We know which kids.

4. Use the for unique or special things

Use the when there is only one in the context, or when grammar makes the noun specific.

  • the sun, the moon, the capital
  • the best student because superlatives use the.
  • play the guitar because musical instruments often take the.

5. Use the for common places in town

We often use the with common public places that people normally go to.

  • the bank, the station, the park
  • the library, the cinema, the doctor

6. Use no article for general meaning

Use no article before plural nouns or uncountable nouns when you mean the whole idea in general.

  • Children need sleep. Children in general.
  • I love music. Music in general.
  • I do not drink coffee. Coffee in general.

7. Use no article with meals, languages, subjects, and dates

These common categories usually take no article when used normally.

  • breakfast, lunch, dinner
  • English, biology, math
  • Monday, May, 2026

8. Watch special contrasts

Some words change article use depending on meaning.

  • I watch TV. No article because TV means broadcasting.
  • Turn off the TV. The physical machine is specific.
  • last week means the week before now, so no article.
  • the last day of the trip means a specific final day.
Final quick rule: a/an means one general thing, the means a specific or known thing, and zero article often means general plural or uncountable meaning.
