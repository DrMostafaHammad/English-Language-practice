Brief Explanation

These are the basic word-order rules practised in the game.

Subject + Verb

Most English sentences need a subject before the verb: She works, They live here. Use it or they when the subject was already mentioned.

Verb + Object

The object comes after the verb. Keep the verb and object close together: I like music very much, not I like very much music.

Frequency Adverbs

Words like always, often, and usually usually go before the main verb: We often cook. With be, they go after it: She is always early.

Place + Time

When a sentence has both place and time, English normally puts place first, then time: We met at the cafe yesterday.
